The Initiative
Behind BarKaDa

BarKaDa (Barangay Kalinisan Day) is a community-driven initiative in Barangay Tagapo that promotes environmental cleanliness and public health. Through regular clean-up drives, waste segregation efforts, and educational campaigns, BarKaDa encourages residents to take active roles in keeping their surroundings clean and sustainable. It reflects the spirit of unity and shared responsibility for a healthier barangay.

Lamok Buster Team

Lamok Buster is a community-driven and responsive project that aims to protect public health. It is one of Barangay Tagapoâ€™s main environmental health programs in order to stop mosquitoes from spreading, particularly during the rainy season when mosquito populations tend to increase.

Lamok Buster Equipments
The Lamok Buster team utilizes equipment and chemicals intended to eradicate mosquito breeding grounds. Residents can also request mosquito spraying services for their houses or communities.
Lamok Buster Operations
The Lamok Busters team regularly fogs and performs daily cleaning operations as preemptive measures. Their efforts are extended throughout the barangay including empty lots and vacant areas.

Kaminero ni Etaw

Clean-Up Drives is regularly organized by Kaminero ni Etaw as a means to keep the neighborhood clean and deal with typical problems like flooding and waste buildup. Every Saturday, these drives primarily concentrate on cleaning manholes, drainage systems, canals, and rivers, particularly in areas that are vulnerable to flooding.

Kaminero ni Etaw

Through daily canal cleaning, creek declogging, scheduled clean-up drives, and regular grass cutting, they play a vital role in reducing flood risks, promoting public health, and fostering a cleaner, safer, and more resilient neighborhood.

Their consistent efforts not only protect waterways and public spaces but also inspire active community involvement in environmental care.

Material Recovery Facility

This project is about segregating wastes in places at Tagapo like Anros, Buena Rosa, and Cataquiz where wastes that are still useful are set aside. This MRF is sustainable by minimizing environmental harm, innovative in its approach to transforming waste into resources, and impactful through its ability to empower the community and inspire long-term behavioral change.

  • Sustainable
  • Innovative
  • Impactful

Mindful Segregration,
Clean Communities

Barangay Tagapo operates waste segregation sites in Anros, Buena Rosa, and Cataquiz. The cleaning team oversees the sorting of recyclable and reusable materials to reduce environmental waste.

The MRF helps reduce pollution, supports waste management, and promotes recycling as a potential livelihood opportunity. It also serves as a model for other communities aiming for cleaner and more sustainable waste practices.
